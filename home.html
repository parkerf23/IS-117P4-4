DC's History

Founded in 1934 as "National Allied Publications", by Malcolm Wheeler-Nicholson. Running out of funding Wheeler pushed to join forces with magazine distributors Harry Donenfeld and Jack Liebowitz to create Detective Comics in 1937.

Golden Age

The Golden Age ushered in the world's mightiest heros with Superman (1938) in "Action Comics," Batman (1939), and Wonder Woman (1941). This age notably introduced the costumed superhero genre to the world.

Silver Age

The superhero genre took a huge hit in the 1950s. It wasn't until writer Robert Kanigher and artist Carmine Infantino revealed the new look for the Flash. After this, the Silver Age was born and over the next few years, the entire DC roster got an overhaul.

The Multiverse

DC's multiverse contains a plethera of universes existing in the same physcial space, but kept apart by differing vibrational speeds. "In the beginning, there was only one. A single black infinitude...so cold and dark for so very long...that even the burning light was imperceptible. But the light grew and the infinitude shuddered...and the darkness finally...screamed, as much in pain as in relief. For in that instant a multiverse was born. A multiverse of worlds vibrating and replicating...and a multiverse that should have been one, became many." - The Monitor
